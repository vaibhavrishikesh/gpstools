# Market Landscape

## Demand is real and large
Geotag camera apps collectively have **hundreds of millions of installs**. Two apps alone (gonext 21M + GPS Map Camera 130M) = 150M+, plus dozens of clones. India is a primary market.

## Two distinct user segments

### 1. Casual / B2C
Travel, memories, social sharing. Wants: pretty location stamps, weather, map view, easy share. Low willingness to pay. Monetized via ads. **Crowded and commoditized.**

### 2. Field-proof / B2B-ish (the money segment)
Construction site progress, real estate documentation, agriculture/survey, NGO/government fieldwork, insurance, audits, attendance proof. Wants:
- **Tamper-evident** timestamp + GPS + address baked into photo
- **Accuracy** (correct coordinates, NavIC in India)
- **PDF / report export** as proof of work
- Batch capture, offline maps, custom fields (project name, employee ID)
- Reliability — these users depend on it for billing/compliance

This segment **pays** and is **underserved on quality** (leader sits at 3.34★).

## Monetization patterns
- Free + ads (baseline; what gonext does).
- **Freemium IAP:** remove ads + advanced stamp customization (GPS Map Camera model).
- **Subscription** for reports, cloud backup, team features — highest LTV; top apps stack 2–3 models. One-time + subscription combo lifts conversion 15–25%.
- B2B angle (Timemark/Conota) → higher per-user value, "photo evidence" framing.

## The gap / opportunity thesis
1. **Quality gap:** leader has 130M users but 3.34★ — complaints about ads, accuracy, bloat, crashes. A clean, fast, reliable app can peel off users.
2. **Trust gap:** field users need defensible proof; many apps feel cheap/spammy. Brand a *trustworthy* geotag camera.
3. **India edge:** NavIC, Hindi + regional languages, regional templates.
4. **Design edge:** our SharePoster work (templates, rendering, polish) transfers directly to beautiful, customizable stamps — a differentiator in an ugly category.

## Risks
- Extremely crowded; ASO is hard against established keywords.
- Camera + GPS accuracy is non-trivial engineering (device fragmentation).
- Low casual willingness-to-pay; real revenue needs the B2B/report angle.
- Big incumbents can copy features fast.
